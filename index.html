The show

What we get into

Every conversation comes back to the same question: what will it actually take to build robots that are useful in the real world? Three ideas run through all of it.

Learning in context

The real world is never fully specified in advance, so a useful robot has to read the situation in front of it and adjust.

Self-calibration

Hardware drifts and environments shift. The systems that win are the ones that tune themselves without a human stepping in.

Data and scale

The breakthrough will not be one clever idea. It will be enormous amounts of real interaction data, scaled hard.

About

I'm a tech investor and a non-technical robotics fan, and I started this show because the biggest engineering story of the decade is being told in a language most people can't follow.

Embodied HQ is for people who are smart but not specialists. Founders, operators, investors, engineers from other fields, and anyone who can feel that robotics is about to matter and wants to actually understand it.

I bring on the people building these systems and ask them to explain their work plainly. No hype, no doom, and no pretending the robots that don't work aren't just as interesting as the ones that do.
